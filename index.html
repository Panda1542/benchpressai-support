Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains what BenchPress AI processes or collects, how the information is obtained, why it is used, where it is stored, when it is shared, and the choices available to users.

1. On-Device Camera and Computer-Vision Processing

BenchPress AI requests camera permission so it can analyze bench press movement, count repetitions, estimate bar path and speed, evaluate tilt or balance, and generate workout feedback.

Live camera frames are analyzed locally on the user's device using Apple's AVFoundation, Vision, and Core ML frameworks. Raw camera frames, photos, videos, body-pose landmarks, and form-analysis results are not sent to the BenchPress AI developer, OpenAI, ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or any other third-party AI service.

BenchPress AI does not use a third-party AI service. The word "AI" in the app name refers to automated on-device computer-vision analysis.

Raw live camera data is not retained after analysis unless the user deliberately creates or exports a highlight, screenshot, or saved posture snapshot through an app feature. User-created exports remain under the user's control and are shared only when the user chooses to share them.

2. Workout and Performance Data

The app may create and store workout information such as:

  • Weight
  • Repetition count
  • Workout date and time
  • Total volume
  • Estimated one-repetition maximum
  • Bar speed and bar path
  • Bar tilt and balance feedback
  • Form or grind scores
  • Ranks, streaks, progress, rewards, packs, or cards
  • Session reports and saved posture snapshots

This information is used to provide workout tracking, reports, history, progress, ranks, rewards, and related app features. It is stored locally on the device unless the user separately gives permission to enable Apple CloudKit features.

3. Optional Apple iCloud and CloudKit

Apple CloudKit is the only optional cloud service used by BenchPress AI. CloudKit is not required to use the app's local workout features.

Before CloudKit is enabled, the app displays the exact data involved and requires the user to select an explicit permission checkbox. The user may instead select Continue without iCloud.

Data sent to the user's private Apple CloudKit database after permission

  • Workout sessions and history
  • Saved posture snapshots contained in session history
  • Progress data, owned reward cards, unopened packs, and rank state
  • A trial/access ledger containing free reps used, sample completion time, prior verified Pro access, clock-integrity information, and update timestamps
  • The user's CloudKit record identifier, used to associate private records with the user's Apple Account

The private database is used for cross-device sync and recovery. Private CloudKit data is not displayed to other BenchPress AI users.

Data sent to the app's public Apple CloudKit database after permission

  • The user's chosen leaderboard display name
  • Rank and score
  • Streak
  • Total repetitions
  • Best or heaviest weight
  • Estimated one-repetition maximum
  • Total volume
  • Form score
  • Set or session count
  • Update time

Public leaderboard information can be seen by other BenchPress AI users. Users should not use a real name, sensitive personal information, or other private information as a leaderboard display name.

Data never uploaded to CloudKit

  • Raw live camera frames
  • Raw workout video
  • Apple Account password

Apple processes CloudKit information under Apple's privacy, security, and service terms. BenchPress AI relies on Apple to provide privacy and security protections at least equivalent to the protections described in this policy for data handled through CloudKit.

4. Local Device Storage and Keychain

Workout history and app settings may be stored locally on the device. The app also stores a limited trial/access ledger in the iOS Keychain to prevent repeated use of one-time free access. This ledger may include free reps used, the sample completion time, prior verified Pro access, clock-integrity information, and update timestamps.

Keychain information can remain after a normal app deletion. If the user enables iCloud, the trial/access ledger may also be stored in the user's private CloudKit database for recovery across devices or reinstalls.

5. Subscriptions and Purchase Status

Purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store and StoreKit. BenchPress AI does not receive the user's full payment-card number.

The app checks Apple StoreKit subscription entitlement and verified transaction history to unlock Pro features, restore purchases, and determine whether prior free or Pro access has been used. A limited purchase/access status may be included in the private CloudKit trial/access ledger when the user has enabled iCloud.

6. Support Communications

This information is used to respond to the request, investigate problems, handle privacy or deletion requests, and maintain appropriate support or legal records.

7. Device Information, Diagnostics, Advertising, and Tracking

The app does not include third-party advertising, cross-app tracking, or third-party analytics SDKs. BenchPress AI does not sell personal information.

Apple may process App Store, purchase, crash, or device diagnostics according to the user's Apple settings and Apple's own policies. BenchPress AI may receive information made available by Apple to developers for app operation, purchase verification, crash investigation, or App Store administration.

8. How Information Is Used

Information is used only as needed to:

  • Provide on-device bench press analysis and workout feedback
  • Count reps and generate workout metrics
  • Save local workout history and progress
  • Provide optional cross-device sync through private Apple CloudKit
  • Operate the optional public leaderboard

  • Prevent repeated trial or sample abuse
  • Respond to support and privacy requests
  • Maintain security, reliability, and legal compliance

9. Data Sharing

BenchPress AI does not share personal data with a third-party AI service.

When the user explicitly enables iCloud, the app shares the specific private and public data listed in Section 3 with Apple CloudKit. Public leaderboard data is also visible to other BenchPress AI users. Information may additionally be disclosed when required by law, necessary to protect users or the service, or necessary to respond to a valid legal request.

10. Data Retention

Private CloudKit information may remain while iCloud sync is enabled and until it is deleted through the app, the user's Apple Account or iCloud controls where available, or a verified deletion request.

Public leaderboard information may remain until it is replaced, removed, or deleted following a verified request. Trial/access information may be retained as reasonably necessary to enforce one-time access and prevent abuse. Support emails may be retained for customer service, security, dispute resolution, and legal recordkeeping.
